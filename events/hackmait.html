01

16 Hours of creating prototypes that innovate on provided themes. Collaborating to code, develop and deploy innovative solutions!

Come, hack MAIT !

Rules

To check your innovative mind and coding skills grab the opportunity of "HACKMAIT 3.0" This will comprise of two rounds, where participants need to register in teams of maximum 4 members. Description of each round is given below.

Online Round

In this round participants will be provided with themes on the platform. After individual registration and team formation, they are required to submit their project ideas based upon the mentioned themes and upload them on the same platform before the deadline i.e. by 12 pm on the 13th March. The results of Round 1 will be sent to the shortlisted teams via email within 1-2 days. The selection process is based on both the originality and usefulness of the idea submitted. Selected participants are required to build their prototype in the offline rounds.

Offline Round

The selected teams will have to build their hack on campus. They will be given 12 hours to build their prototype on campus and they can continue working at home.

19 March 2020- Day 1: The participants have to report at the campus at 9am. The hack will start at 10 am. All the participants will start working on their prototypes. The teams will have till 4pm to code and then will go back home and continue coding if required.

20th March 2020 (Day2): The participants will have to report to the campus at 9am. They will get time till 12pm to finish their hack. A mentor evaluation round will be conducted where 10(?)teams will be shortlisted for the pitching round. Pitching will start at 1:30(?) where the judges will evaluate the hacks. Pitching will be followed by result announcement.

FAQ's
Q) What is a hackathon?

A Hackathon is an intense event that brings together people like software developers, graphic designers and user interface specialists along with industry process experts and professionals to identify issues and create software solutions.

Q) What do I need to bring to the event?

This is a very hands-on event. Bring your laptops and mobile phones, but above all, your energy and your brain!.

Q) How many members can a team have?

A team can have anywhere between 2-4 members. 2 being the minimum number and 4 being the maximum number of members.

Q) Can students from other colleges participate?

YES. Anyone from anywhere can be a part of impulse and HACKMAIT 3.0.
